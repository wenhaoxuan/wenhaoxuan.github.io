About me

I am a fourth-year PhD student and NASA FINESST Fellow at Caltech.

My primary research interest lies in exoplanet atmospheres. Specifically, I am working on direct imaging and high-resolution spectroscopy to measure the compositions, spins, and radial velocities of giant planets.

Before starting my PhD, I pursued a MPhil in Astronomy at the University of Cambridge (2020) as a Downing Scholar. There, I worked with Mark Wyatt on calculating the 3-D orbits of dynamical masses of giant planets discovered by the radial velocity technique.

I received a BA in Physics from Pomona College (2019), where I spent 4 enriching years learning about the universe, widening my worldview.

Research Projects

Completed and ongoing research

TESS

Advised by Dimitri Mawet, Heather Knutson, Jason Wang, and Jean-Baptiste Ruffio

I study KPIC spectra (R~35,000) of directly imaged giant planets and brown dwarfs companions to measure their atmospheric compositions, rotation rates, and radial velocities. In the first paper on brown dwarf HD 4747 B, I demonstrated that high-resolution spectra yielded consistent results independent of assumptions on clouds, while low-resolution data of the same object fail to robustly constrain its abundances.

I am currently working on a sample of eight young, planetary-mass companions to uniformly measure their abundances and constrain their formation pathway.

Direct measurements of planetary orbital mutual inclinations

Advised by Mark Wyatt

I'm also interested in combining different methods (imaging, radial velocity, astrometry) to constrain 3D orbital architectures and masses of exoplanets. I started this work during my MPhil at Cambridge.

Our results on π Men and HAT-P-11 in Xuan & Wyatt 2020, and results on the two debris disk systems in Xuan et al. 2020b.

Characterizing the Keck/NIRC2 vortex coronagraph (2017-2018)

Advised by Prof. Rory Barnes and Dr. Rodrigo Luger

In the summer of 2017, I wrote software to automate the data processing workflow for the Keck/NIRC2 vortex coronagraph, and helped set up a Mongo database and a Flask website to organize and display the data products. These tools are still in use by the group.

With the pipeline, I re-reduced archival data of more than 300 stars, and wrote a paper (Xuan et al. 2018) on characterizing the instrument's performance.
